# FFmpeg Editor - User Guide

This guide will help you get started with the basic and advanced features of the FFmpeg Desktop Editor.

## 1. Importing Media

To start editing, click the **"Click to open a media file"** area on the landing screen. You can select any supported video or audio format.

## 2. Basic Playback

Use the video player to preview your file:
- **Play/Pause:** Click the play button or video area.
- **Seeking:** Drag the slider below the video to jump to a specific time.
- **Volume:** Use the volume slider or mute button to control audio.

## 3. Trimming Video

Precise trimming is easy with the visual timeline:
- **Visual Timeline:** The blue overlay shows the selected region.
- **Numerical Input:** Enter exact start/end times in the inputs below the timeline.
- **Preview:** The selection duration is updated in real-time.

## 4. Applying Video Filters

Before exporting, you can enhance your video:
1. Click **Export Settings**.
2. Scroll to the **Video Effects** section.
3. Adjust sliders for:
   - **Brightness:** Make the video lighter or darker.
   - **Contrast:** Increase or decrease color difference.
   - **Saturation:** Make colors more vibrant or grayscale.
   - **Blur:** Soften the video elements.
   - **Sharpen:** Enhance edge detail.
4. Click **Reset** to return to original settings.

## 5. Exporting Your Work

1. Click **Export Now**.
2. Choose a destination folder and file name.
3. Select your **Output Format** (MP4, MKV, WebM, etc.).
4. Choose a **Quality Preset** (High, Balanced, or Small Size).
5. (Optional) Choose a **Resolution** to scale the output.
6. Click **Export Now** again to start the process.

## 6. Batch Processing

If you have many files to convert with the same settings:
1. Configure your settings in the **Export Settings** dialog.
2. Click **Add to Queue** instead of Export Now.
3. The file will appear in the **Batch Queue** at the bottom of the main screen.
4. Once you've added all files, click **Start Batch Process**.

## 7. Merging Multiple Clips

1. Click the **Merge Clips** button.
2. Click **+ Add More Files** to select the videos you want to join.
3. Ensure they are in the correct order.
4. Click **Merge X Files**.
5. Choose an output location.

> **Tip:** For the fastest merging, ensure all clips have the same resolution and codecs.

## 8. Burning Subtitles

1. In the **Export Settings** dialog, click **Browse** under the Subtitles section.
2. Select an `.srt`, `.vtt`, or `.ass` file.
3. The subtitles will be permanently "burned" into the video during export.
